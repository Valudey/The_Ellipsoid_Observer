Machines reading comprehension development to monitor social media. Problem: How to recognize irony?

With the ban of Trump from Twitter and a lot of other social media we are a lot more akin to speak about the subject of whether the platform is responsible for its content or not. And, to monitor its platform, Twitter and a lot of other social media opted for machine comprehension (reading or vocal) coupled with human check. But can we completely remove the human part in all that process? That’s the question we are trying to develop today.

The biggest problem against this development according to me is irony. Ans more specifically in machine reading comprehension. Irony is the process of telling something while making understand others that we mean the opposite. And in oral comprehension it’s more or less easier to catch because of the tone of the person. But when the sentences are written, it become much more difficult because you need to know the person to catch the irony. So, an AI would need to learn to know each people of the platform, and to be able to adapt itself to them. That is a big problem because the AI would need a humongous lot of computing power to do that.

To remediate this, we could separate people in groups by personality and adapt the AI to each of the groups. I think research is needed in that field, and is probably already being conducted in a lot of lab.

Special art exhibition taking place at the Tate Modern that will blow your perspectives and get your mathematical perception upside-down

The mathematics arrive at the Tate Modern for a period of three months. Many artists and mathematicians will exhibit their works from March 3. We will notably find in this exhibition Matt Enlow, Stephen Kenny, Aaron Moran...

In his color pencil drawing “Nine Point Circle and Euler Line for an Acute Angle”, Kenny explores the concept of complex geometric constructions to create straight-edge wedges of color. A Nine Point Circle is constructed in reference to a particular triangle. The nine points consist of: the midpoints of each of the three sides, the foot of each of the three altitudes, and the mid point between each vertex and the orthocenter (the point where the three altitudes intersect).

Different series, different formats, different colors, with his wooden structures, Aaron Moran is playing with perspectives to create a 3D vision of a 2D work.

Paris Agreement under the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change could lead to a development of the measures against the Greenhouse gases

The Paris Agreement on climate change is the first universal, legally binding global climate agreement. It was signed on 22 April 2016 and ratified by the European Union on 5 October 2016. This agreement aims to strengthen the global response to the threat of climate change, including by limiting global warning to well below 2°C.

Obviously, to reach that objective, this agreement will help reduce the greenhouse gases emission such as CO2. To do that the agreement key point are:

  • preparing and applying national plans to meet the targeted reduction;
  • reporting to each other and to the public on the progress they are making on their commitments;
  • conducting a planet-wide stocktake every 5 years, from 2023, with international partners, to set further targets based on scientific evidence and the results achieved;
  • taking measures to deal with the impact of climate changes that are already unavoidable;
  • providing practical and financial support to developing countries, to help them adapt to climate change.
Hopefully for us this agreement will improve our situation.
